Regulatory Compliance

Our commitment to meeting and exceeding industry standards

1. Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA) Compliance

P2P Transport maintains full compliance with all FMCSA regulations, including:

  • DOT Number: 12345678 (Active and in good standing)
  • MC Number: MC-987654 (Interstate operating authority)
  • USDOT Registration: Current and regularly updated
  • Safety Rating: Satisfactory (as determined by FMCSA)

1.1 Hours of Service (HOS) Compliance

  • Electronic Logging Device (ELD) mandate compliance
  • Driver duty time monitoring and enforcement
  • Regular audits of driver logs and records
  • Automated alerts for HOS violations

1.2 Vehicle Maintenance and Inspection

  • Annual DOT vehicle inspections
  • Preventive maintenance programs
  • Daily vehicle inspection reports (DVIR)
  • Immediate repair of safety-related defects

2. Driver Qualification and Training

All P2P Transport drivers meet or exceed federal qualification standards:

2.1 Driver Requirements

  • Valid Commercial Driver's License (CDL)
  • Clean driving record (MVR) review
  • DOT physical examination and medical certification
  • Drug and alcohol testing compliance
  • Background check and employment verification

2.2 Ongoing Training Programs

  • Annual safety training and certification
  • Defensive driving courses
  • Hazmat training and certification (when required)
  • Customer service and professional conduct training

3. Insurance and Financial Responsibility

P2P Transport maintains comprehensive insurance coverage as required by federal regulations:

3.1 Liability Coverage

  • Public Liability: $1,000,000 minimum coverage
  • Cargo Insurance: $100,000 per occurrence
  • Environmental Restoration: As required for hazmat transport
  • General Liability: Comprehensive business coverage

3.2 Financial Responsibility

  • BMC-91 and BMC-91X endorsements on file
  • Regular financial audits and reporting
  • Bonding requirements for freight broker operations

4. Environmental Compliance

P2P Transport is committed to environmental stewardship and regulatory compliance:

4.1 Emissions Standards

  • EPA SmartWay certified partner
  • Modern fleet meeting current emissions standards
  • Fuel efficiency monitoring and improvement programs
  • Idle reduction policies and equipment

4.2 Hazardous Materials

  • Hazmat endorsement requirements for drivers
  • Proper hazmat classification and documentation
  • Emergency response procedures and training
  • Compliance with Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA)

5. State and Local Compliance

We maintain compliance with regulations in all states where we operate:

5.1 Permits and Registrations

  • International Registration Plan (IRP) for vehicle registration
  • International Fuel Tax Agreement (IFTA) compliance
  • Oversize/overweight permits when required
  • State-specific operating permits and authorities

5.2 Tax Compliance

  • Quarterly IFTA fuel tax reporting
  • Heavy Vehicle Use Tax (HVUT) payments
  • State and local tax obligations
  • Worker compensation insurance

6. Data Protection and Privacy

P2P Transport complies with applicable data protection regulations:

6.1 Customer Data Protection

  • Secure handling of customer shipment information
  • Electronic data interchange (EDI) security protocols
  • Privacy policy compliance and implementation
  • Data breach notification procedures

6.2 Employee Data Management

  • Driver qualification file maintenance
  • Secure storage of personnel records
  • Compliance with employment law requirements
  • Background check and drug testing record keeping

7. Quality Management System

P2P Transport maintains a comprehensive quality management system:

7.1 Certifications and Standards

  • ISO 9001:2015 quality management certification
  • Customs-Trade Partnership Against Terrorism (C-TPAT) certified
  • Transportation Security Administration (TSA) compliance
  • SmartWay environmental partnership

7.2 Continuous Improvement

  • Regular internal audits and assessments
  • Customer feedback integration
  • Performance metrics monitoring
  • Corrective and preventive action procedures

8. Reporting and Transparency

We maintain transparency through regular reporting and communication:

8.1 Regulatory Reporting

  • Quarterly and annual regulatory filings
  • Incident reporting to appropriate authorities
  • Safety performance monitoring and reporting
  • Environmental impact reporting

8.2 Customer Communication

  • Certificate of insurance provision
  • Safety performance data sharing
  • Compliance documentation access
  • Regular compliance updates and notifications
